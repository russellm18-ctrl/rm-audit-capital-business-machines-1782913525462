// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your site references Sharp copiers, wide-format printers, managed IT, and water dispensers as distinct product lines, but if your product and service images carry no descriptive alt-text, no structured JSON-LD LocalBusiness or Product schema, and no geo-tagged EXIF data tying assets to Olympia, WA, search engines index those images as null content. You are bleeding the query "Sharp copier dealer Olympia WA" to competitors whose image files are named, tagged, and schema-wrapped to answer that exact question. Every product photo on cbm-wa.com that loads without structured metadata is a dead asset pulling zero search weight.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Government agencies, school districts, and municipal offices in the Thurston County area—exactly the institutional buyers your 70-year tenure should own—will not sign a managed IT or managed print contract based on a text claim alone. Your site states a four-hour average response time, which is a powerful operational differentiator, but there is zero photographic proof: no technician on-site, no service call documentation, no real client environment shown. An institutional procurement coordinator evaluating vendors sees a word count where a credential should be, and clicks away to whoever shows the work.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A sharp regional competitor deploying Sharp or Konica Minolta lines in the greater Puget Sound corridor is running geo-tagged environmental photography—actual installs inside named business categories like law firms, medical offices, or county agencies—paired with Google Business Profile photo stacks that dominate local map pack visibility. You have a 1952 founding date and a licensed Sharp dealership, two hard-won credibility anchors, and neither is represented by a single visual asset that places your equipment inside a recognizable Olympia-area workspace. That gap hands fleet replacement contracts directly to competitors who simply show up visually.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured asset capture across your core service categories—managed IT infrastructure installs, wide-format print output, conference room AV setups, and water dispenser deployments—photographed inside actual client environments with explicit client permission, then deploy every file under a strict naming and metadata protocol: descriptive filenames, alt-text written to match high-intent local queries, GPS-embedded EXIF tied to Olympia and Thurston County, and JSON-LD markup connecting each image to its corresponding service or product schema. This converts your existing service delivery footprint into a searchable, trust-building visual record that works continuously across organic search, your Google Business Profile, and every sales proposal your team sends.
